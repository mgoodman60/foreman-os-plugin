# Meeting Templates Reference

## OAC Meeting Agenda Template

**Owner-Architect-Contractor (OAC)** meetings are the primary project coordination forum. Held weekly or bi-weekly, they require a consistent structure to ensure all critical topics are addressed.

### Agenda Header Block

```
PROJECT: ___________________________________  MEETING NO.: OAC-___
ADDRESS: ___________________________________  DATE: _______________
OWNER:   ___________________________________  TIME: _______________
GC:      ___________________________________  LOCATION: ___________
ARCH:    ___________________________________  NEXT MEETING: _______
```

### Standard OAC Agenda Sections

**1. Call to Order / Roll Call**
- Record all attendees, organizations, and roles
- Note absentees and confirm distribution list is current
- Confirm quorum for decisions (per contract or project procedure)

**2. Safety Moment**
- Brief safety topic (2–3 minutes), rotating responsibility by meeting
- Reference any recent incidents, near-misses, or toolbox topics
- Record topic covered for documentation

**3. Approval of Previous Minutes**
- Review action items from prior meeting
- Accept corrections or revisions
- Formally adopt minutes as written (note approval in record)

**4. Schedule Update**
- Current project phase and overall percent complete
- Milestone status (on track / at risk / delayed)
- Critical path items — any float consumed or recovered
- 2-week lookahead summary: planned activities and manpower
- Recovery plan status if schedule is behind

**5. Budget Update**
- Original contract value and current contract value
- Approved change orders to date (count and dollar value)
- Pending COs / PCOs in negotiation (dollar exposure)
- Owner contingency remaining
- Current pay application status (submitted, in review, approved, paid)
- Projected cost at completion vs. budget

**6. RFI Status**
- Total open RFIs (with average age in days)
- RFIs closed this period
- New RFIs submitted this period
- Critical RFIs blocking work — identify and expedite
- RFIs aging beyond contract response time — flag for owner/architect action

**7. Submittal Status**
- Submittals pending review (with submittal log excerpt)
- Submittals approved this period
- Submittals rejected or requiring resubmission — impact assessment
- Critical-path submittals with required approval dates
- Long-lead items — material delivery status against approved submittals

**8. Change Order Status**
- New Contractor Change Order Requests (CORs) submitted this period
- Proposal Change Orders (PCOs) currently in negotiation — status and dollar value
- Change orders executed this period — contract value impact
- Disputed items and dispute resolution status

**9. Quality / Inspections**
- Recent inspection results (pass/fail, agency, date)
- Upcoming required inspections — schedule and notification requirements
- Open Non-Conformance Reports (NCRs) or deficiency items
- Punchlist status if in closeout phase

**10. Old Business / Action Items**
- Review all carry-forward action items from prior meetings
- Status update for each item (completed, in progress, overdue)
- Reassign or revise due dates as needed
- Escalate overdue items per escalation protocol

**11. New Business**
- New topics raised by any party
- Decisions needed from owner or design team
- Upcoming owner decisions with deadlines (material selections, VE decisions, scope changes)
- Third-party issues (utility companies, inspectors, adjacent owners)

**12. Next Meeting**
- Confirm date, time, and location of next meeting
- Note any agenda items to carry forward
- Confirm distribution of these minutes — target: within 48 hours

## Pre-Install Conference Template

Held before a major trade or specialty contractor mobilizes. Ensures all parties understand requirements before work begins. Record in meeting minutes and retain in project files.

**Scope:** _______________________ **Spec Section(s):** _____________
**Drawing Sheets:** _______________ **Date:** ______________________
**Attendees:** GC Superintendent, Trade Foreman, PM, QC Manager, Design Rep (as needed)

**1. Scope Review**
- Define exact scope boundaries — where this trade's work begins and ends
- Reference spec sections and drawing sheets governing the work
- Confirm exclusions and interface responsibilities with adjacent trades

**2. Material Submittals**
- List all submittals required for this scope and current approval status
- Confirm no work proceeds with unapproved submittals (except as authorized)
- Identify any substitution requests and approval status

**3. Delivery Schedule**
- Material delivery dates and lead times — confirmed with supplier
- Designated staging and laydown areas on site
- Receiving procedures: inspection requirements, rejection criteria, documentation

**4. Quality Requirements**
- Key quality standards from specifications (tolerances, finishes, ratings)
- Hold points — work that cannot proceed without inspection/approval
- Witness points — inspections the GC or owner's rep will observe
- Mock-up or sample requirements and approval process

**5. Safety Requirements**
- Trade-specific hazards (e.g., confined space, energized systems, overhead work)
- Required PPE for this scope beyond site minimums
- Fall protection plan requirements (leading edge, holes, openings)
- Hot work permit requirements and fire watch protocol
- Hazardous materials handling (if applicable)

**6. Coordination**
- Interface with other trades — sequence and dependencies
- Areas where this trade's work must be complete before others can proceed
- Shared access, scaffolding, or equipment use agreements
- Laydown and material storage areas assigned

**7. Testing / Inspection**
- Required tests: type, frequency, who performs, who witnesses
- Inspection notification lead times (24 hours, 48 hours — per spec)
- Who receives test reports and turnaround time requirements
- Consequences of failing tests — retesting, repair, replacement procedures

**8. Weather Restrictions**
- Temperature limits for installation (minimum ambient, substrate, material)
- Moisture or humidity limits (relative humidity, surface moisture)
- Wind speed limits (e.g., roofing, spray applications, crane picks)
- Rain/precipitation restrictions and cure time requirements
- Cold weather or hot weather protection plan if conditions may be encountered

**9. Closeout Requirements**
- As-built drawings — trade responsibility and format requirements
- O&M manuals — sections required, format, quantity of copies
- Warranties — manufacturer and workmanship, duration and coverage
- Attic stock — quantities required per spec
- Commissioning and startup documentation requirements

## Progress Meeting Template

Weekly or bi-weekly field progress meeting, typically superintendent-led with trade foremen and PM in attendance.

**1. Two-Week Lookahead Review**
- Compare planned activities from last lookahead to actual accomplishments
- Identify variances and root causes (manpower, material, weather, coordination)
- Confirm next two-week lookahead is distributed and understood by all trades

**2. Manpower Report**
- Crew counts by trade for current week
- Planned manpower for next two weeks — sufficient for schedule?
- Staffing changes, new subcontractor mobilizations, demobilizations

**3. Material Status**
- Deliveries received this week — any issues (damage, shortages, wrong material)
- Deliveries expected next two weeks — confirmed with suppliers
- Material issues blocking work — expediting actions taken

**4. Quality Issues**
- Open NCRs — responsible party, corrective action, target close date
- Rework items identified this week
- Upcoming inspection schedule — who needs to be notified and when

**5. Safety Updates**
- Any incidents or near-misses this week — brief description, corrective action
- Safety observations (positive and negative)
- Upcoming safety topics and planned toolbox talks
- Any new hazards introduced by upcoming scope

**6. Schedule Recovery**
- If project is behind schedule: recovery actions in place, projected recovery date
- Overtime or additional crew being deployed
- Trade sequence adjustments to recover float

**7. Action Items**
- New action items generated from this meeting
- Assign owner, due date, and priority to each item
- Read back all action items before closing the meeting
