# Weekly Report Language Standards

## Audience
Weekly reports are executive-facing documents for owners and project managers. The tone is professional, confident, and summary-oriented — not field-operational.

## Core Principles
1. **Third person, past tense** for accomplished work
2. **Summarize, don't enumerate** — aggregate daily details into weekly narrative
3. **Lead with accomplishments** — positive momentum first, challenges second
4. **Use contract terminology** — reference milestones, spec sections, contract clauses
5. **Quantify progress** — percentages, counts, durations, not vague language
6. **Address challenges with solutions** — never present a problem without a mitigation plan

## Transformation Examples — Daily to Weekly

| Daily Report Language | Weekly Report Language |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| "Poured 45 CY concrete at east wing footings" | "Foundation concrete placement progressed at the east wing, with 180 CY placed across the week. Footings F1-F8 are now complete (67% of total foundation concrete)." |
| "Walker had 6 guys, Metro had 8 guys" | "Average daily headcount of 42 workers across 6 active subcontractors, consistent with planned mobilization levels." |
| "Rain Tuesday morning, couldn't work outside" | "A half-day weather delay on Tuesday (0.5" precipitation) was offset by accelerated interior work, resulting in no net schedule impact for the week." |
| "Inspector failed the rebar spacing" | "A structural inspection identified one rebar spacing correction in the east footing. The correction was completed same-day and re-inspection passed Wednesday." |

## Vocabulary Preferences

| Avoid (Field Language) | Use (Executive Language) |
|-----------------------|------------------------|
| "guys" / "workers" / "dudes" | "crew members" / "tradespeople" / "workforce" |
| "behind schedule" | "tracking [X] days behind baseline; recovery plan in place" |
| "messed up" / "screwed up" | "required correction" / "deviation identified" |
| "waiting on" | "pending receipt of" / "awaiting" |
| "no-showed" | "did not mobilize as scheduled" |
| "got rained out" | "weather delay impacted exterior operations" |
| "passed" / "failed" | "inspection approved" / "inspection identified deficiencies" |

## Milestone Language
- "Substantial Completion is targeted for [date], currently tracking on schedule."
- "[Milestone] was achieved on [date], [X] days ahead of/behind baseline."
- "The critical path currently runs through [activity], with [X] days of total float."

## Variance Explanations
Always include: what happened, why, impact, and mitigation.
"The mechanical rough-in experienced a two-day delay due to a design coordination issue identified during field installation (RFI-012 submitted 2/15). The architect's response was received 2/18, and work resumed 2/19 with no impact to the overall schedule due to available float."

## Tone Calibration
- **Not alarming**: "Schedule risk identified" not "We're going to be late"
- **Not defensive**: "Recovery actions initiated" not "It wasn't our fault"
- **Not vague**: "3 weather delay days this month" not "some weather issues"
- **Not boastful**: "Milestone achieved on schedule" not "We crushed it"
